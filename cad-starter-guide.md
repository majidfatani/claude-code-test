# Beginner CAD Tools Starter Guide

A quick-start guide to the top 3 CAD tools for beginners interested in 3D printing.

## 1. Tinkercad

Tinkercad is a free, browser-based 3D design app made by Autodesk that requires no installation and no prior experience. You simply open it in your browser, drag and drop basic shapes, and combine them to build your model. It's intentionally simple — the interface strips away everything that would overwhelm a newcomer — making it the fastest way to go from zero to a printable STL file. If you've never touched CAD software before, Tinkercad is the right place to start.

## 2. Autodesk Fusion 360

Fusion 360 is a professional-grade 3D CAD, CAM, and CAE tool from Autodesk that is free for personal and non-commercial use. Unlike Tinkercad, it uses parametric modeling, meaning your design is built from dimensions and constraints that you can edit at any time — a huge advantage when you need precise parts for 3D printing. It has a steeper learning curve than Tinkercad, but the payoff is enormous: Fusion 360 is used by hobbyists and engineers alike, has a massive library of tutorials, and will take you as far as you want to go in CAD.

## 3. FreeCAD

FreeCAD is a free, open-source parametric 3D CAD application that runs on Windows, Mac, and Linux. It is community-built and entirely free with no paywalls or subscription fees, making it a great long-term alternative to commercial tools. FreeCAD is well-suited for designing mechanical parts and objects intended for 3D printing, and its active community means plenty of tutorials, forums, and add-ons are available. It can be quirky at times, but for beginners who want a powerful tool without any cost, FreeCAD is a solid choice.
